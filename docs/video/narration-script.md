# Smart Agent Demo — Narration Script

**Voice:** Female, warm, professional, mid-pace (~140 wpm). Friendly but not casual.
**TTS recommendation:** ElevenLabs "Rachel" or "Bella" voice, Stability = 0.5, Clarity = 0.7.
**Total runtime:** ~4:30

---

## INTRO (0:00 – 0:18)

> Welcome to Smart Agent — an intelligent platform for managing organizations with verifiable trust, delegated authority, and AI-powered agents.
>
> In this walkthrough, I'll show you three things: how to log a field activity, how to invite a new team member, and how to explore the generational map that tracks how your work multiplies.

---

## SECTION 1 — LOGIN (0:18 – 0:42)

> Let's start at the home screen. Smart Agent supports multiple communities, each with their own organizations and team members.

*[Pause 1s — landing page is visible]*

> I'll select the Church Planting Movement community.

*[Pause 2s — community card expands]*

> Now I'll log in as Priya, who leads a field team in Kolkata. Notice she's shown as Team Leader.

*[Pause 2s — click Priya, page navigates to dashboard]*

> The dashboard shows Priya's organization at a glance — four members, several partner relationships, and a summary of the team's structure. The org selector in the header lets her switch between organizations she belongs to.

---

## SECTION 2 — LOG AN ACTIVITY (0:42 – 1:50)

> Let's log a field activity. I'll click "Activities" in the navigation bar.

*[Pause 2s — activities page loads]*

> At the top, you can see summary metrics — total activities logged, how many happened this week, total participants reached, and cumulative hours.

*[Pause 2s — point to summary cards]*

> To log a new activity, I'll click the "plus Log Activity" button.

*[Pause 2s — form expands]*

> The form asks for a few key details. First, the activity type — I'll choose "Outreach."

*[Pause 1s — select dropdown]*

> For the date, I'll set today's date.

*[Pause 1s]*

> Participants — let's say eight people were involved.

*[Pause 1s]*

> The title describes what happened: "Neighborhood visit — New Town area."

*[Pause 1s — type title]*

> Location: "New Town, Kolkata."

*[Pause 1s]*

> Duration: about ninety minutes.

*[Pause 1s]*

> And in the notes, I'll add a brief description: "Visited six families in the apartment complex. Two expressed interest in joining a study group."

*[Pause 2s — type description]*

> Now I'll click "Log Activity."

*[Pause 2s — form submits, page reloads]*

> The activity appears at the top of the feed, color-coded by type. You can see it's tagged as "Outreach" in orange, with all the details: who logged it, how many participants, location, and duration.

---

## SECTION 3 — INVITE A USER (1:50 – 3:00)

> Next, let's invite a new team member. I'll navigate to "Organization" in the nav bar.

*[Pause 2s — team page loads]*

> This page shows everyone connected to Priya's team — current members with their roles, related organizations, and AI agents. Scroll down and you'll find the invitation section.

*[Pause 2s — scroll to invite form]*

> To create an invite, I select a role from the dropdown. The roles come from the team's template — Team Leader, Church Planter, National Partner, or Coach.

*[Pause 1s]*

> I'll choose "Church Planter" for our new team member.

*[Pause 1s — select role]*

> Notice the description underneath: "Frontline church planting and discipleship." This tells the invitee exactly what access and responsibilities the role carries.

*[Pause 1s]*

> I'll click "Create Invitation."

*[Pause 2s — invite code appears]*

> A unique invite link is generated. I can copy this link and share it with my new team member — by message, email, or however our team communicates.

*[Pause 2s — highlight the invite URL]*

> When they open the link, they'll see the organization name, the role they're being invited to, and can accept with one click. The system automatically creates their identity on-chain and establishes the trust relationship.

*[Pause 1s]*

> The invite also appears in the pending invites table above, where I can track its status.

---

## SECTION 4 — BUILD THE GENERATIONAL MAP (3:00 – 4:15)

> Now for the most powerful feature — the generational map. I'll click "Gen Map" in the navigation.

*[Pause 2s — gen map page loads]*

> At the top, you see movement-level metrics: total groups, the deepest generation reached, total baptisms, how many groups function as established churches, and the multiplication rate.

*[Pause 3s — point to each metric]*

> Below that is the generation pipeline. This shows how many groups exist at each level: G-zero is the initial contact, G-one is the first groups formed, G-two are groups started by G-one groups — and G-three means the movement is multiplying beyond the original team.

*[Pause 3s — point to generation pipeline]*

> Getting to generation three and beyond is a key indicator that a self-sustaining movement is forming. The work is no longer dependent on the original team.

*[Pause 2s]*

> Below the pipeline is the tree view. Each node represents a group with its generation badge, leader name, location, and health markers.

*[Pause 2s]*

> The health markers track four things: S for seekers — people exploring. B for believers. "Bap" for baptized members. And L for leaders who are being developed.

*[Pause 2s — point to markers on a node]*

> Groups that have started new groups show "multiplied" status in green. Groups marked as "church" have met the basic health markers — regular gathering, giving, and identified leadership.

*[Pause 2s]*

> Each group also has a health score from zero to one hundred, calculated from engagement, discipleship depth, leadership development, and whether the group has started new groups.

---

## CLOSING (4:15 – 4:30)

> That's the core workflow — log what's happening in the field, bring new people onto the team, and watch how the work multiplies across generations.
>
> Smart Agent gives you verifiable, on-chain trust for every relationship and activity — so your data isn't just stored, it's cryptographically secured and auditable.
>
> Thanks for watching.
