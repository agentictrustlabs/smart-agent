## Segment 1 — Intro + Login

Welcome to Smart Agent — a platform for managing organizations with verifiable trust, delegated authority, and intelligent agents.

In this walkthrough, I'll show you how a hub lead logs field activities, invites new facilitators to the team, and explores the generational map that tracks how community circles are multiplying.

I'll select the Catalyst Network community and log in as Linh, who leads a facilitator hub in Da Nang. The dashboard shows her team at a glance — members, relationships, and the hub's structure.

The organization selector in the header lets her switch between the hub she leads and the broader regional network she reports into.

---

## Segment 2 — Log an Activity

Let's navigate to Activities. The summary cards show total activities logged, how many happened this week, participants reached, and cumulative hours.

I'll open the form and log a new outreach activity. The type is Outreach — we had ten participants in Thanh Khe ward, Da Nang. Duration was ninety minutes.

In the notes: "Introduced the program to ten families. Three expressed strong interest in joining a learning circle."

After submitting, the activity appears in the feed with its type, who logged it, participant count, location, and duration. The whole team can see what's happening in the field.

---

## Segment 3 — Invite a Facilitator

Now let's bring someone new onto the team. On the Organization page, I can see current members and their roles.

Scrolling to the invite section, I select "Facilitator" from the role dropdown. Each role has a clear description of the responsibilities it carries.

After creating the invitation, a unique link is generated. When the new facilitator opens this link, they see the hub name, the role, and can accept with one click. The system automatically creates their verified identity and establishes the trust relationship.

---

## Segment 4 — Generational Map

This is where the real impact becomes visible. I'll switch to the Mekong Catalyst Network to see the full picture.

The generational map tracks how learning circles multiply. At the top — total circles, deepest generation, participants reached, established circles, and the multiplication rate.

The generation pipeline shows the counts at each level. G-zero is the original pilot. G-one is the first circles formed. G-two are circles started by G-one participants, not by the original team. And G-three means the work is self-sustaining — participants are training new facilitators who start their own circles.

In the tree view, each node shows its generation, the facilitator leading it, location, and health markers. Circles marked "multiplied" have produced new circles. Those marked "established" are self-sustaining with regular attendance and trained facilitators.

Two independent streams are tracked here — you can see exactly where multiplication is happening and where circles need more support.

---

## Segment 5 — Closing

That's the core workflow — log what's happening in the field, bring new people onto the team with role-based access, and track how the work multiplies across generations.

Every activity and relationship is recorded with verifiable, on-chain trust — not just stored in a database, but cryptographically secured and auditable.

Thanks for watching.
