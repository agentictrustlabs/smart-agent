# Smart Agent Demo — Trupeer Narration Script

corresponding part of the screen recording.

**Voice:** Female, warm, professional
**Pace:** Moderate (~140 words per minute)

---

## Segment 1 — Intro + Login

Welcome to Smart Agent — an intelligent platform for managing organizations with verifiable trust and AI-powered agents.

In this walkthrough, I'll show you three things: how to log a field activity, how to invite a new team member, and how to explore the generational map.

I'll start by selecting a community. Each community has its own organizations and team members.

I'll pick a team leader role — notice the organization name and role displayed on the card.

The dashboard gives an overview of the team at a glance: members with their roles, AI agents, and the organization template. The org selector in the header lets me switch between organizations.

---

## Segment 2 — Log an Activity

Let me navigate to Activities. At the top you can see summary metrics — total activities, how many this week, total participants, and cumulative hours.

To log something new, I'll open the form. It asks for a few key details.

Activity type — I'll choose Outreach. Then the date, number of participants, a short title describing what happened, the location, how long it lasted, and any notes.

After submitting, the activity appears at the top of the feed, color-coded by type. Everyone on the team can see it — with the who, what, where, and when all captured.

---

## Segment 3 — Invite a Team Member

Now let's invite someone new. On the Organization page, I can see current members and their roles.

Scrolling down to the invite section, I select a role from the dropdown. The roles come from the organization's template — each one has a clear description of what it includes.

After creating the invitation, a unique link is generated. I can share this with my new team member. When they open it, they'll see the organization and role, and can join with one click.

---

## Segment 4 — Generational Map

This is the most powerful feature. The generational map tracks how groups multiply across generations.

At the top, movement-level metrics: total groups, the deepest generation reached, baptisms, established groups, and the multiplication rate.

The generation pipeline shows counts at each level. G-zero is the initial contact. G-one is the first groups formed. G-two are groups started by G-one. And reaching G-three means the work is multiplying beyond the original team — a self-sustaining movement is forming.

Below is the tree view. Each node shows its generation, the leader, location, and health markers — seekers engaging, believers growing, people baptized, and leaders being developed.

Groups marked as "multiplied" have started new groups. Those marked as a "church" have met all the basic health markers.

Multiple independent streams are tracked in one view, so you can see the full picture of where multiplication is happening and where it needs attention.

---

## Segment 5 — Closing

That's the core workflow — log what's happening, bring new people onto the team, and watch how the work multiplies across generations.

Every activity and relationship is tracked with verifiable trust — not just stored, but cryptographically secured and auditable.

Thanks for watching.
