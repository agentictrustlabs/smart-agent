# Catalyst Network Extended Demo — Trupeer Narration

**Voice:** Female, warm, professional (~140 wpm)
**Total narration:** ~6 minutes across 10 segments

---

## Segment 1 — Intro + Login (0:00)

Welcome to Smart Agent — a platform for managing organizations with verifiable trust and intelligent agents.

In this walkthrough, I'll take you through the full workflow — from the dashboard analytics to logging field activities, managing your team, and building out the generational map that tracks how your work multiplies across communities.

I'll select the Catalyst Network and log in as Linh, who leads a facilitator hub in Da Nang.

---

## Segment 2 — Dashboard Analytics (0:20)

After logging in, the dashboard shows organization-level analytics. I'll switch to the regional network view to see the full picture.

At the top, summary cards show members, relationships, AI agents, and the organization template.

Scrolling down, we see the activity trend chart — showing weekly activity count and participant reach over the last eight weeks. Next to it, a breakdown by activity type shows the mix of outreach, training, meetings, and coaching.

The multiplication panel tracks the generational map metrics — total circles, deepest generation, how many are established, and the multiplication rate.

Below that, a recent activity feed shows the latest field reports from the team.

---

## Segment 3 — Activity Feed (1:00)

Let me switch to the Da Nang Hub to see the team's activities. On the Activities page, summary cards show total activities, how many happened this week, total participants reached, and cumulative hours.

The activity feed shows each entry with its type badge, title, who logged it, participant count, location, and duration. Every entry can be edited or deleted.

---

## Segment 4 — Calendar View (1:20)

I'll switch to the calendar view. Each day shows activities as colored tags. You can see at a glance which days were active and which types of work happened. Clicking any activity opens it for editing.

---

## Segment 5 — Log a New Activity (1:35)

Let me log a new activity. I'll switch back to the feed view and open the form.

Type: Visit. Participants: six. Title: "Follow-up visit — Thanh Khe families."

For the location, Thanh Khe in Da Nang. Duration: seventy-five minutes.

In the notes: "Visited three families from last week's outreach. The Hien family wants to host a weekly learning session. The Thanh family introduced us to neighbors."

After submitting, it appears at the top of the feed. The whole team can see the update.

---

## Segment 6 — Edit an Activity (2:15)

I can also edit existing activities. Clicking "Edit" on an entry opens the form pre-filled with its data. I'll update the participant count and save.

---

## Segment 7 — Team + Invite (2:30)

On the Organization page, I can see all team members with their roles and status. Scrolling down, there's a section for inviting new facilitators.

I'll select the "Facilitator" role — it comes with a clear description of what the role includes. After clicking "Create Invitation," a unique link is generated. When the new person opens this link, they see the hub name, the role, and can accept immediately. The system creates their verified identity and establishes the trust relationship automatically.

---

## Segment 8 — Generational Map Overview (3:10)

Now the signature feature — the generational map. I'll switch to the Mekong Catalyst Network to see the full picture.

At the top, movement-level metrics: total circles, deepest generation — which is G-three — established circles, and the multiplication rate.

The generation pipeline shows counts at each level. G-zero is the original pilot. G-one is the first circles formed. G-two are circles started by G-one participants. And G-three means the work is self-sustaining — participants are training their own facilitators.

---

## Segment 9 — Build the Map — Add a Node (3:50)

Let me add a new circle to the map. I'll click "Add Child" on an existing node.

The form asks for the circle name — "Hoi An Circle" — the leader, "Bao Nguyen," and the location, "Hoi An."

Below that, health markers track the group's progress. I'll enter five prospects, three active participants, and one certified facilitator.

Notice the circle preview — a dashed outline means it's still a gathering. When I check "Established," it becomes a solid circle, indicating the group is self-sustaining.

After saving, the new node appears in the tree connected to its parent.

---

## Segment 10 — Edit a Node + Closing (4:40)

I can also edit existing nodes. Clicking "Edit" opens the form with the current data. I'll mark this group as established and note that it has started one new group.

The circle preview updates — solid outline, with the "groups started" count in the center.

After saving, you can see the updated tree reflecting the change. The multiplication metrics update too.

Scrolling through the tree shows the full picture — two independent streams, multiple generations deep, with health markers on every node showing exactly where the work is thriving and where it needs attention.

That's the complete workflow. Log field activities, switch between feed and calendar views, manage your team with role-based invites, and build out the generational map interactively — watching your work multiply across communities.

Every activity and relationship is recorded with verifiable, on-chain trust. Thanks for watching.
